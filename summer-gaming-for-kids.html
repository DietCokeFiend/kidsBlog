Gaming Distractions for the Summer

Written by Grandmaster Q. on May 21, 2013.

With summer already here, some of you have enrolled your kids in some sort of summer camp.For those who have missed the deadline or for whatever reason have not found a way to escape the constant terror of your kids, you may have to resort to other methods of losing them.

During the day, you may take them to the various events or activities available around the Greater Toronto Area. What about when they get home? What do you do?

The video game industry over the years have grown trememdously. With mobile gaming being a big factor in the gaming industry today, it is very easy for kids to pick up a tablet or smartphone and dive right into the plethora of free games available.

Here we have compiled a list of games that will keep your kids distracted for a while.

  • Angry Birds
  • Where's My Water
  • Fruit Ninja
  • Temple Run

Angry Birds

by Rovio Entertainment

Angry Birds have become a force to reckon with ever since its release. Since then, there have been several iterations which includes a Star Wars version. The objective of the game is to slingshot angry birds to topple structures and/or to crush various enemies. Warning: this game is highly addictive and is recommended for everone.

  • Developer: Rovio Entertainment
  • Price: Free for selected platform (PC/IOS/Android)
  • Rated: Everyone

Where's My Water

by Disney Games

This is a game developed and published by Disney. The premise is quite simple. Mr. Aligator needs his water to take a shower or bath. It is your goal to clear the path so that water can get to Mr. Aligator's water pipes. I've seen kids play with this and it's hours upon hours of clicking and tapping fun on any of the prominent mobile devices.

  • Developer: Disney Games
  • Price: Free (PC/IOS/Android)
  • Rated: Everyone

Fruit Ninja

by HalfBrick Studios

Fruit Ninja is a very popular addictive game created by HalfBrick. The premise is also very simple. Fruit is thrown in the air. It is up to you to slice them all before they drop out of the screen, all the while avoiding the bombs and creating epic combos. The name of the game is getting the highest score. Adorable graphics and easy to pick up gameplay makes this one addicting game for adults and kids alike. There are free versions and paid versions for both IOS and Android.

  • Developer: HalfBrick Studios
  • Price: Free and Paid (IOS 0.99US / Android 1.22US)
  • Rated: Everyone

Temple Run

by Imangi Studios

Simple controls and infinite gameplay makes this one game hard to resist for your little ones. Run through a course, avoid obstacles and collect the gold! Did I mention the controls are simple? Only four possible moves: flick up to jump, down to slide, left to move left and right to move right. Free for both IOS and Android

  • Developer: Imangi Studios
  • Price: Free (IOS/Android)
  • Rated: Everyone

There you have it, folks. Great games to distract entertainment your little ones for a bit.
